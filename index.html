♦  Approach  ♦

Data is gathered to insure all project designs reflect a keen understanding of the unique challenges that face individuals, teams, and organizations.

All work is based on enduring principles and researched methodologies that are grounded in human motivation theory, management science, organizational systems theory, and ethical guidelines.


Designs are created in collaboration with leaders and managers to produce and implement programs that are relevant and sensitive to the client's culture.

♦  Service Details  ♦

Leadership Development

Strong leadership and management are essential elements of organizational success.
I engage individuals in a process to discover and articulate his/her unique leadership talents and address potential blind spots. A series of one-on-one meetings include discussions and exercises that help develop insight and strategies to bring one’s best self to leading and managing.
Researched methods in professional development and skill building are thoughtfully delivered. The process is grounded in human motivation, learning and systems theories and includes: assessment, goal setting, executive coaching, measurement of progress and the establishment of strategies for continued learning.

Areas of focus:

  • Personal vision, values and goals
  • Developing effective business relationships
  • Strategy development
  • Leading and managing change
  • Leveraging Performance Reviews
  • Delegating
  • Succession planning

Team Development

The ability to quickly form and reform productive teams is a critical skill for businesses today. The groundwork necessary to form productive teams is often overlooked.

Assistance is provided to newly formed teams, existing teams and teams in transition due to mergers and other organizational changes. Programs are designed in collaboration with team leaders to insure that the experience is relevant, and helps the team produce clear tools and deliverables to take back to the work place.

Designs often include:

  • Development of a team's vision and goals
  • Clarification of team member roles and responsibilities
  • Exploration of the diverse work styles of each team member
  • Development of team communication and planning skills
  • Development of team conflict management and problem solving skills
  • Improvement of working relationships among team members

Organizational Developmewnt

Common scenarios in business today include: reorganizations, leadership transitions and adjustments due to corporate growth and downsizing. Change might also be initiated by leaders to improve the organizations performance or culture. Regardless of how changes are introduced, organizations must respond thoughtfully and quickly.

Work is done with leaders, managers, and supervisors to successfully manage the human aspects of change initiatives. Clients are assisted in planning, communicating and facilitating change efforts that minimize resistance and encourage support and collaboration.

These services often include:

  • Education regarding change and change management
  • Leading, planning and goal setting
  • Communication strategies
  • Reducing resistance and gaining support from key players at all levels
  • Roles and responsibilities of key players
  • Methods to avoid loss of productivity during change
  • Methods to lower employee stress during change
  • Methods to acknowledge and celebrate achievements

♦  Bio  ♦

Patricia Keegan Ed.D

Owner / Executive Coach

Patricia M. Keegan Ed.D

I am an executive coach, consultant and psychologist with over 20 years experience working with individuals, teams and organizations. My work is primarily focused on improving leadership, management, communication and performance.

Client industries have included: high-tech, biotech, pharma, healthcare, higher education, financial services, architectural and design services, and transportation. I enjoy working in both the private and public sectors ranging from large Fortune 500 organizations to non-profits and small entrepreneurial start-ups.

I also enjoy consulting with and coaching individuals at all levels of the organization. I groom high achievers as they pursue the next level or work with derailed executives to help them reconnect with their purpose and reach their potential. As a psychologist and former educator I bring to each project an understanding of interpersonal and leadership styles, human motivation and organizational systems.

Before becoming a Senior Vice President at ClearRock, Inc., I co-founded the management consulting partnership of Keegan and Kanter Associates, a firm focused on executive coaching, team development and organizational change. In my earlier background, I was an Assistant Professor at Boston University and a licensed Senior Psychologist at Brookline Psychological Services.

What motivates all my work is my desire to assist people and their organizations in achieving thoughtful and constructive change. I believe in asking the right questions. I am pragmatic and results oriented in my approach and quickly engage clients in their own learning and development.


Education, Training and Professional Activities

  • I earned a Doctorate of Education in Human Development and a Masters of Education in Counseling Psychology from Boston University My Bachelor of Science in Education is from the University of Maine
  • My work is influenced by my study and practice in Mindfulness. I have completed over 50 continuing education credits from Harvard Medical School in this area
  • I am an active member of the Human Resource Leadership Forum
  • I enjoy sharing my experience and expertise as a Supervisor for Executive Coaching Candidates at William James College
  • I have been the Chair of the Advisory Council for Future Chefs, a non-profit serving at risk inner city teens and young adults for over 5 years
